Character Profile

Hari Seldon

The Psychohistorian

Artist's Analysis: Hari is shown as calm, thoughtful, and highly intelligent. The pose and styling should make him feel like a man who is already thinking several steps ahead, which matches his role as the creator of psychohistory.

Importance: Hari Seldon is the person who starts the Foundation’s entire plan. His predictions and ideas shape the future of the story, and his actions show how one person’s knowledge can influence thousands of years of history.

Character Profile

Gaal Dornick

The Observer

Artist's Analysis: Gaal should feel young, curious, and a little uncertain. A softer style would fit his role as someone who is just beginning to understand the size of the events around him.

Importance: Gaal helps introduce the reader to the Foundation’s larger story. He gives a view of the collapse of the Empire and shows how ordinary people become part of huge historical changes.

Character Profile

Salvor Hardin

The Pragmatist

Artist's Analysis: Salvor should look grounded, confident, and practical. The artwork can use stronger contrast to show that he is direct and willing to make hard choices when the Foundation needs stability.

Importance: Salvor Hardin is important because he turns the Foundation toward strategy and politics instead of only science. He shows that intelligence, timing, and diplomacy can be the tools that keep a civilization alive.

Character Profile

Hober Mallow

The Merchant

Artist's Analysis: Hober should feel sharp, confident, and a little worldly. The artwork can use strong warm colors and a steady expression to suggest someone who understands trade, power, and persuasion.

Importance: Hober Mallow matters because he represents the Foundation’s rise through trade. His story shows that business and commerce can become just as powerful as armies when shaping the future.
