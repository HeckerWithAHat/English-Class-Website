Author Profile

Isaac Asimov

Born

1920

Isaac Asimov was a prolific author and professor of biochemistry, best known for the Foundation series and the Three Laws of Robotics.

Background

Early Life & Background

Origin

Petrovichi

Isaac Asimov was born in Petrovichi, Russia, and emigrated to the United States at the age of three, settling in Brooklyn, New York. He worked in his family’s candy store and developed an early interest in science fiction after discovering pulp magazines. A gifted student, Asimov attended Columbia University and earned degrees in chemistry and biochemistry, later working as a civilian chemist during World War II.

Career

Career & Achievements

Genre

SciFi

Asimov’s writing career began when he sold his first story in 1939. He published many seminal works — including the Foundation series and I, Robot — and helped define the Golden Age of Science Fiction. His work combined scientific insight with humanistic storytelling and remains influential across literature and popular culture.
