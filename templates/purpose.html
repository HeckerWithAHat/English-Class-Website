The Purpose of the Author

Foundation draws inspiration from many sources, and is heavily influenced by Asimov's life and interests. But why did he write Foundation?

Azimov Foundation is a novel that isn’t here just to illustrate a message or story of space and adventure. It is a novel that explores the idea of how civilizations come to both rise and fall throughout the times. Throughout the story, we see how many empires are able to grow into power, yet at the same time are ill equipped in maintaining their power.

Nations and empires face inner corruption and civil conflicts, leading to unrest and distrust. The book emphasizes knowledge, innovation, and curiosity as factors to ensure survival. The novel also argues a different message, that history isn’t changed by single heroic individuals, but rather by the masses. That history is influenced by what society does thus the reason “psychohistory” is only able to predict what the masses will do in the future, not an individual.

The novel also warns us against the dependence of others, pulling from the novel. Planets begin to rely upon the foundation, of which ultimately hands their fate, as their only way of surviving is determined based upon the foundation. Azimov shifts the idea of science fiction from just sci-fi battles, towards core issues of which are relatable to even modern-day civilization.
